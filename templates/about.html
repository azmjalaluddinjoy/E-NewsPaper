Why E-NewsPaper !

Traditionally the newspapers is printed in the news pages and customer buy it. Ours have a concept on making it as E-NewsPaper that makes a solution, possible for publishers to effortlessly publish their content in a digital newspaper format. Considering the rapid growth and evolving trends in the digital media market, we want to upgrade it as online newspaper platform to the latest HTML technology which allows publishers to take their digital editions to superior levels and provide a great reading experience to their users. This solution provides a reading experience that is flexible, accurate, and secure. All at an affordable cost for publishers.

E-NewsPaper Publishing Features

01 Ours proposed UI (user interface): We are offering a reliable device presentation layout. This presentation will support on - smartphones, desktops and tablets. Media integration, such as text, images, animation files, automated Email alerts to subscribers once a new edition is uploaded; notification email will be sent to the subscribers. The software permissions to add Banner, Advertising spaces to generate ad revenues. Integration of free registration / login process (for restricted access to the edition, if required).

02 Compatibility: Able to access with any device, anywhere, and anytime.

03 Social Sharing: The content of the article can be shared easily.

04 Zoom In and Zoom out: Zoom functionality will be available on article window.

05 Language Support: Our E-NewsPaper interface supports two languages (Bangla & English).

06 Ad Placement: Our E-NewsPaper Provides a wide range of ad spaces at the page level, article level.

07 Analytics: Using Google analytics we can analyze & understand our audience.

08 Familiarity: Some readers feel more comfortable with the familiar layout of the printed edition. So, our E-NewsPaper will be as same layout of the printed layout.

09 Mobility: If the digital edition of a daily newspaper downloaded to a laptop or other mobile receiving device, it can be read independently of where the user is located, even without permanent internet access.
